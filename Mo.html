About me

I'm Mohammed Obaidullah I'm a

Mohammed Obaidullah is a key governmental advisor and award-winning producer, director, writer, and actor with over 20 years of international experience in the film and television industry. Currently, he serves as the Advisor on Art Riyadh City and is driven to transform the city into a top-10 global entertainment destination by 2030. Mohammed began acting in theater while completing his mechanical engineering degree. Ultimately, he decided to pursue a career in the film and television industry. He directed and produced four short films, including "When Jackie Met Saleh," which won the prestigious Indie Film Festival Award of Merit (Biography & Filmography) in California. Mohammed also worked on two feature films: "Defendor,” starring Oscar-nominated Woody Harrelson, Kat Dennings, and Sandra Oh; and “A Beginner’s Guide to Endings,” starring Oscar winner J.K. Simmons, Oscar-nominated Harvey Keitel, Tricia Helfer, and Scot Caan. As a film industry expert, Mohammed is now directing Saudi Arabia's entertainment sector's growth and development. He has served in several critical roles, including as Executive Director of Strategy and Planning for the General Commission for Audiovisual Media and Chairman of the Cinema Advisory Board. Under his guidance, the country opened cinemas for the first time in its history. He is also working with the Regulatory Development Department to create a compliance strategy and regulations for the media market.
